## 💻 Uses

Inspired by [UsesThis](https://usesthis.com) and [Uses.tech](https://uses.tech) I like to keep track of my current developer/entrepreneur setup.

### Hardware

**Framework Desktop, 16-core/32-thread AMD Strix Halo CPU with integrated Radeon 8060S GPU, 128GB RAM (2026)** Although I keep the Macbook Pro around (for developing a couple of internal Mac apps), my daily driver is now a Linux desktop, currently running Omarchy although I may move to running regular Arch Linux now I've mostly moved away from Omarchy tools to self-managed forks/replacements.

**14" MacBook Pro, M1 Max, 64GB RAM, 1TB SSD (2021)** I had an absolute beast of an Intel Macbook Pro before this one, upgrades to the Apple Silicon version and it was like night and day. I don't think I'd go back to an Intel chipset, and definitely wouldn't move away from MacOS back to Windows or Linux - I just love the awesome user experience combined with high level of customisability, and don't think either of those competitors comes close.

**Iiyama G-Master G4380UHSU Red Eagle** My favourite monitor I've ever had - 43" 4K 144Hz. Previously I used a 43" Super-ultrawide monitor which was great, but towards the end I felt like it wasn't good for consuming content, whereas a traditional 4:3 (4K) monitor would be great for both screen real estate for developing, and for consuming content. I debated with myself for a while about getting one of the larger brands, but my first ever PC monitor was an Iiyama and I always felt at the time like they punched above their price point. I've had this monitor since about March (it's July at the time of this update) and I love it.

**Logitech MX Mechanical Mini** My mechanical keyboard has an incredible battery life. I switched from a Keychron K1 which I always left plugged in because the battery would become weird with random disconnects after a week or two, but this Logitech lasts literally over 6 weeks before it needs an overnight charge. I spent so much of my life developing on Apple keyboards that it was strange when I first started using a mechanical keyboard again, like the days of old. But I really like the clicky sound and couldn't see me going back now.

**MX Master 3S** After so long using an Apple Trackpad, I switched to this mouse for both gaming on my gaming machine and development. Using the same hardware for both really helps me not have to change context.

**Insta 360 Webcam** Having tried a bunch of different cameras, the Logitech C920 most recently before this, this is finally the one that I'm most happy with the quality on. And in today post-Covid world with 100% remote work, a decent camera setup is quite important.

**Yamaha HS3 driven by Scarlett Solo** These speakers sound incredible and were a great replacement for my Logi Z407s previously. On Mac the volume slider natively doesn't work so you have to use the volume knob on the Scarlett Solo, but under Linux it works great.

**Apple Airpods Pro & Apple Airpods Max** For audio on the move I tend to switch between those two. If it's for a quick "I'm waiting for my car to be serviced" the Airpods Pro are my goto choice. But if it's a long flight to USA or Asia, then the Maxs come out. To be fair I love both for their own benefits, but the Maxs are definitely my top choice (because of great battery life and noise cancelling).

**Gaming PC** As this is is just for fun, I'll put it in a single line. I use the same monitor for my Mac setup, same keyboard and mouse. The internals are a Ryzen 7 5700X3D, 32GB DDR4 RAM and a 4080 Super GPU. A considerable replacement for my previous Intel i7 8700K, 16GB of RAM, GeForce 1080Ti 11GB of RAM. Gaming wise, most recently has been Arc Raiders, but Apex Legends is still probably my most favourite game of recent years.

**Apple iPhone 16 Pro 256GB** I love iPhones but being honest they've got to the point where upgrades are less important as I use my phone less and less (some WhatsApp, some Facetime, X and Tiktok are really about it).

**Apple iPad Pro 11" 2026 256GB** My wife and I have had iPads since the very first version and when we upgraded to the Pros in 2018 I worried that we'd be on just a more expensive upgrade cycle. However, it turned out that the Pro simply resists the need to upgrade for much longer (our 2018 ones lasted until 2026, way longer than I'd have expected). Most commonly used apps are Concepts for wireframing and Documents for eBooks, along with YouTube Premium. It's definitely mostly a content consuming device more than content creation.

### Software

**Neovim** In the past I've used Sublime Text, TextMate, Atom and VS Code, but I'm after switching to Neovim on my Mac last year, I quickly felt more productive in it than I did in VS Code. That made my switch to Linux on the desktop much more feasible.

**Claude Code** Having tried AI coding assistants first in about November 2025 and finding them to be useless, like a junior pair programmer little brother that kept doing the wrong thing, the jump in about February 2026 was night and day. It was at the point where it could definitely replace junior developers. Since then until now (written in July 2026) I've improved my prompting ability and now admit I'm an AI-first developer. With a decent prompt/spec, I can get an AI solution for even the most adventurous of features, or legacy site rewrites/upgrades to be 90+% complete, then with some tight feedback loops, generally get it over the line without needing to edit much code. So I'd say my role as a developer has already changed to be AI-wrangler and code-reviewer.

**Brave** Lots of people prefer Chrome, Firefox or (dare I say it) Edge, but while I used to be 100% on Safari, on the desktop I use Brave now I've moved to Linux. The ad-blocking combined with Chrome extensions is a winning combination, and with 128GB of RAM I'm never worried about increased memory usage.

**1Password** All my passwords are synced between devices using 1Password (I've been a happy customer of them since 2008!). I'm looking forward to making more use of Passkeys going forward as they become ready.
